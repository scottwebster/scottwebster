Scott and his girlfriend Mairi are undertaking an overland mountaineering journey from Scotland to Mongolia, with several mountaineering objectives along the way, including attempting to climb Khan Tengri (7010m), one of the most beautiful mountains in the world.

Team

Scott Webster is one of the UK’s youngest qualified mountaineering instructors, passing his MIA qualification aged 21. Scott has spent a total of 3 years living and working in Antarctica, during which time he has led scientific and logistic parties throughout the Antarctic Peninsula, the Ellsworth Mountains, Ronne Ice-Shelf and Pine Island Glacier. Scott has been involved in several large climbing and mountaineering expeditions in the past to Alaska, Greenland, Norway and the European Alps.

Mairi Fenton studied Marine and Freshwater Biology at the University of Glasgow before joining the British Antarctic Survey. She has spent a total of 3 years in Antarctica as a marine biologist – a job that includes diving (under the ice in winter) and small boat sampling. She loves travelling and has spent time living and working abroad as well as exploring places including Borneo, Nepal, Greenland and New Zealand. An outdoors enthusiast - she is as at home in the mountains as the sea!
